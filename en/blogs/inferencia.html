Statistical Inference

How to move from sample observations to population-level conclusions

Article 6 of 7 · Statistical Foundations

Inference is not about certainty. It is about disciplined uncertainty quantification.

Population vs. Sample

The population is the full target system. The sample is what you observed. Inference estimates population parameters from sample statistics.

Point Estimates and Uncertainty

Every estimate carries variability. Means, proportions, and model effects must be reported with uncertainty context.

Confidence Intervals

Confidence intervals describe plausible ranges for population parameters under repeated sampling assumptions.

They are often more decision-useful than isolated p-values.

Practical Takeaways

  • Sampling quality defines inference quality.
  • Always pair estimates with uncertainty measures.
  • Use interval thinking when communicating decisions.
