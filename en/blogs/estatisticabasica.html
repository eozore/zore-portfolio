Basic Statistics

Mean, median, dispersion, and what your data may be hiding

Article 2 of 7 · Statistical Foundations

Reporting only one metric can produce the wrong business decision. Statistics is useful when measures are interpreted together.

Central Tendency

Mean summarizes average behavior but is sensitive to outliers.

Median is robust for skewed data and often better for income, price, and latency distributions.

Mode highlights the most frequent value and is useful in categorical analysis.

Dispersion

Variance and standard deviation quantify spread.

IQR (Q3 - Q1) captures the central 50% and supports outlier detection with Tukey's rule.

Quartiles and Percentiles

Quartiles segment data into four parts; percentiles into one hundred. This is key for customer segmentation and risk tiering.

In credit, churn, and fraud use cases, percentile-based thresholds are often more reliable than absolute thresholds.

Practical Takeaways

  • Use median with skewed distributions.
  • Track spread metrics, not only central metrics.
  • Use percentiles for operational and risk segmentation.
