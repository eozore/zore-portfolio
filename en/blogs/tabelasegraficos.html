Tables and Charts

How to turn numbers into decisions for technical and executive audiences

Article 3 of 7 · Statistical Foundations

The right chart answers the right question. The wrong chart can hide risk just as much as poor statistics.

Choosing the Right Visual

Histogram: distribution shape, skewness, and tails.

Boxplot: robust comparison across groups with outlier detection.

Bar chart: categorical comparison.

Line chart: trends over time.

Scatter plot: variable relationships and correlation exploration.

When to Use Tables

Charts are better for pattern recognition; tables are better when the reader needs exact values for decision making.

A practical approach is: chart for insight, table for action.

Practical Takeaways

  • Define the analytical question before selecting a visual.
  • Avoid pie charts when precise comparisons matter.
  • Use both visual and tabular views in executive reporting.
